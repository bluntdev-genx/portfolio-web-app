## Design Context

### Users
Hiring managers, recruiters, product/data leaders, and collaborators evaluating Ashutosh Patel's data full-stack, analytics engineering, and data product experience. They need to understand impact quickly, inspect resume artifacts, and see evidence of product-minded analytics work without navigating a marketing site.

### Brand Personality
Premium, precise, and operational. The interface should feel like a refined portfolio crossed with a data command center: calm enough for professional review, sharp enough to signal technical depth, and specific enough to avoid generic AI-generated presentation.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light editorial base inspired by the Wealth Management Experience: warm off-white surfaces, firm black rules, restrained gold accents, strong serif headline moments, and polished product copy. Layer in dark command-center panels inspired by the Infrastructure Self Serve Experience: compact metrics, pipeline/log language, monospaced labels, and status-driven interactions. Avoid neon-heavy dark mode, generic gradients, blobs, glassmorphism, and repeated icon-card grids.

### Design Principles
- Show product work as operational evidence: problem, system, metric, and outcome.
- Keep the first viewport useful: name, current positioning, quantified impact, and direct resume/contact actions.
- Use motion sparingly for comprehension: reveal hierarchy, count metrics, and show command-state transitions with reduced-motion support.
- Treat resumes and portfolio content as one source of truth to prevent drift.
- Preserve professional density while preventing text overlap, cramped mobile states, and decorative filler.
